Get Up to Speed | Honda Civic : The History
Honda Civic | First Generation

Year of Production: 1972-1978

Engine: 1,169 cc four-cylinder water-cooled engine

Transmission: 5 Speed Manual

Designer: Hiroshi Kizawa, Shinya Iwakura

This was an automobile of its time, arriving shortly before a global oil crisis. Deceptively small: the rear has less space than the superminis that were becoming popular in Europe, like the Fiat 127 and Renault 5. With its smooth gearbox, splutter-free tiny engine, and light steering (though unsettlingly vague around straightahead), it was a breeze to drive. The American model demonstrated Honda's engine prowess by passing California clean-air regulations without a catalytic converter thanks to its CVCC four-cylinder.

Honda Civic | Second Generation

Year of Production: 1979-1982

Engine: 1488 cc EM CVCC Inline 4

Transmission: 4 or 5 Speed Manual and 3 Speed Automatic

A hatchback has a more angular appearance and contemporary conveniences! gearbox with five speeds! Internally movable door mirrors! The fundamental package was still there. However, purchasers in the US and even Europe requested additional room because the inside was still small. Honda swiftly took action and mercilessly replaced it after only four years. There were also five-door, wagon, and saloon models in this generation. If you're having trouble picturing the saloon, keep in mind that it was also manufactured in Cowley as the Triumph Acclaim. Leyland benefited from having a car that didn't break apart as you drove it home from the showroom, while Honda benefited from avoiding European import restrictions.

Honda Civic | Third Generation

Year of Production: 1983-1986

Engine: 1590 cc ZC Inline 4

Transmission: 4 or 5 Speed Manual, 3 or 4 Speed Automatic

This one, particularly the tall estate model now known as Shuttle, was a little roomier and wider. The Civic's design became more angular once more in the vernacular of all Japanese automobiles from the 1980s. The steering was at last as much fun as the other controls thanks to a rack-and-pinion arrangement. Gen 3 included the Shuttle, a four-door saloon, a three-door hatch, and the car you care about, the CRX, a small, sporty coupe. You have the right to be less concerned about the related Rover 213 saloon (Triumph had been put to death; Honda and Rover were now properly in bed).

Honda Civic | Fourth Generation

Year of Production: 1987-1990

Engine: 1.6 L B16A Inline 4

Transmission: 4 or 5 or 6 Speed Manual, 4 Speed Automatic

Here's another that lasted only four years, since rapid model turnarounds became the standard in the Japanese bubble economy. The double-wishbone suspension that lowered the bonnet line made the three-door larger and lower once again, and the sheetmetal was softer and more attractive than before. This time, Honda's powertrain expertise was once again demonstrated by the CRX's rev-crazy VTEC engine, which made it a true classic.
